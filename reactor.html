Reactor hall

The KTH Reactor Hall (Reaktorhallen), officially known as R1, was Sweden's first nuclear reactor. Inaugurated in 1954, it is located 25 meters underground in a cavern blasted out of the bedrock directly beneath the KTH Royal Institute of Technology campus in central Stockholm.

The reactor, which was used for research in nuclear physics and chemistry, was decommissioned in 1970. After being completely dismantled and decontaminated, the unique and dramatic subterranean space has been transformed. Today, the Reactor Hall serves as a one-of-a-kind venue for cultural events, art installations, concerts, and performances, representing a fascinating intersection of scientific history, Cold War-era architecture, and contemporary arts.
